## Pavilion (joinpavilion.com)

## WHAT WE'RE BUILDING
A single, self-contained HTML page for Pavilion's newest (and cheapest) membership tier: **Digital Passport**. The page is a prototype to test the proposition — sendable to members or prospects. Includes a feedback popup at ~35 seconds with 3 quick questions.

- **$588/yr** (save $240)
- Cancel anytime

## WHAT DIGITAL PASSPORT IS

- Access to a Slack community (unless we get rid of Slack)
- Ability to connect with others digitally
- Ability to get content pushed and pulled
- An alive, vibrant digital resource (a partner) for navigating being an in-seat operator
- Ask questions from peers
- Benchmark studies (cost nothing, fully subsidized)
- See what people are talking about
- Should have more in common with **Netflix or Amazon Prime** than traditional Pavilion in how we position it

---

## THE CORE EMOTION

**Not FOMO. Deeper than FOMO.**

FOMO says: "I'm missing out on something happening right now."
What operators actually feel is: **"The world is moving and I'm standing still."**

The word is **DRIFT** — the fear of drifting slowly, invisibly away from what best-in-class looks like. You don't get fired in a dramatic moment. You just gradually become the person in the room with outdated assumptions.

- Your board asks about AI strategy and you're guessing
- You hired a rep at a comp package you *think* is market but you don't actually know
- You restructured your team the way you did it at your last company, but that was 2022
- A peer at a similar company is growing 2x faster and you can't figure out why

---

## THE VALUE PROPOSITION

**Curated, validated, reputable, credible SIGNAL.**

Every operator is drowning in noise — LinkedIn hot takes, vendor-sponsored "research," podcasts saying opposite things, AI-generated content flooding every channel. The problem isn't access to information. It's **trusting** information.

What we're selling:
- **Credibility** — the data comes from real operators in verified seats
- **Curation** — someone filtered the noise so you don't have to
- **Currency** — it's current, not a 2023 report recycled with a new cover

**"This number is real. This insight is from someone who actually did it. This benchmark came from 10,000 operators, not a blog post."**

---

## THE POSITIONING

Bloomberg Terminal is the *what* (trusted signal you can act on).
Netflix is the *feel* (effortless, obvious, slightly addictive — of course you have this).

The language should be short, confident, almost casual:
> **Know what good looks like.**
> Real comp data. Real benchmarks. Real operators. Updated constantly.
> $69/mo. Cancel anytime.

Netflix energy — we're not begging you to join. We're telling you what it is.

**"Every operator you respect already has this open in a tab."**

Pavilion positioned not as a community you join, but as a **tool you use**. Nobody says "I'm a member of Netflix." They say "I use Netflix."

---

## THE EMOTIONAL PROMISE

**"Stay current. Stay sharp. Stay dangerous."**

The best operators don't guess. They check. This is where they check.

## VISUAL DIRECTION (V2 — NOT YET BUILT)

User direction: **"Playboy (heyday icon) with the smartness of The Economist and the coolness of the Waverly Inn."**

- Has SOME connection to Pavilion brand but STRETCHES that boundary to a place that feels more elevated
- Needs to feel bold, proprietary, interesting
- Big creative swings required
- Not a safe SaaS marketing page
- Should feel like a *statement*
- Editorial, dark, typographically bold
- The kind of visual confidence that makes someone feel like they stumbled onto something important

**V1 (the safe version) is built in index.html — it's the clean/bright SaaS approach. V2 needs to be the bold reimagination.**

## PAGE STRUCTURE

1. **Hero** — "Know what good looks like." headline
2. **Social proof bar** — 10,000+ operators, 47 benchmarks, $2.1T combined revenue
3. **Problem** — You're making high-stakes decisions on low-quality signal (noise list)
4. **Signal/Features** — 6 cards: Live Benchmarks, Operator Signal Feed, Verified Playbooks, Peer Access, Curated Content, Always Current
5. **Use Cases** — Board prep, hiring, CEO questions, reorg, gut checks
6. **Pricing** — $69/mo, $588/yr, feature list
7. **Closing** — "The best operators don't guess. They check."
8. **Feedback Modal** — Pops at 35s, 3 questions:
   - How compelling? (1-5 scale)
